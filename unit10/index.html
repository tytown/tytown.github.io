ContraTravel is the award-winning true story of one man's life experiencing the world from a wildly different perspective. The author demonstrates how to truly enjoy the wonders of this planet - in your own unique way.

Experience the ride of living against the norm:

  • Running in the fast, cut-throat field of international banking and trade;
  • Plotting the rescue and recovery of a Hong Kong billionare's investment;
  • Opening and directing a major US State Government office in Japan, reporting directly to a U.S. Governor and former U.S. Attorney General;
  • Encouters with a snake huge enough to swallow a small tribe, while swimming in a Belizean jungle river;
  • Teaching International Economics at the Graduate School of a Major Midwestern University;
  • Running for Congress against the megalithic Bush family political jugnaut;

... and so much more.

What is. . . ContraTravel?

ContraTravel is a way of seeing the world from a unique perspective. A perspective devoid of pressurized travel groups, sightseeing agencies, excursion pimps and the like. Thomas demonstrates how to truly enjoy this great planet and create your own unique experiences on your own terms - at your own discretion.


The book, published in 2015, is of the travel/memoirs genre and and is a selection of unique and interesting travel stories by the author who has travelled to over 200 countries over the past 40 years.


ContraTravel has been elected as a semi-finalist (nonfiction) by the 2015 Tucson Book Festival Literary Awards Committee, from over 500 entries. This festival is the 4th largest in the United States and is held on the campus of the University of Arizona. In 2014 it drew over 130,000 visitors.

Looking back, the following six-month sojourn kicked off what would be years of extensive international travel.
